Last Chance Trio

The Last Chance Trio was given another chance!

For their new release, they decided to pay tribute to some of their favorite jazz composers, arranging the music of Wayne Shorter, Ornette Coleman, John Coltrane, Chick Corea, Thelonious Monk, Kenny Kirkland, Cole Porter, and the standard "Stars Fell on Alabama" by Frank Perkins and Mitchell Parish (although some of the arrangements didn't make the cut).


The Last Chance Trio's History

The Last Chance Trio is a collective made up of three (!) musicians who at some point called the Bay Area their home.

They met in 2011 when they were all part of the SFJAZZ Educational High School Program, and within the next year they had grown to be close musical friends- at times talking about the Miles Davis Second Great Quintet, at others finding ways to channel the "HeadHunters" musical style.

Around the end of the school year of 2012, they booked a going-away performance because Omree was leaving for college. About a year later, during the summer of 2013, they booked several performances as part of the Marcelo's leaving for college. They didn't know it would be seven whole years before they'd reunite and make music as a group together again.

In 2020, as Marcelo and Omree lived only a few blocks from each other on MacArthur Street in Oakland, CA, they started playing music again, and the last chance trio was given another chance. It was destined that they play more music together. Since Logan was only a few hours away (by car), they decided to bring the group back together and record themselves. The Last Chance Trio is back to doing what they do best with a new release - "Chance".
